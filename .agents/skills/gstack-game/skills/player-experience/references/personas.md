# Player Personas

Each persona has 4 dimensions adapted from hakoniwa's agent modeling framework:
- **Profile** — Who they are, when/where/why they play, emotional triggers
- **Stance** — Default attitude toward the game (supportive → skeptical spectrum)
- **Behavior Type** — How they process and react (MBTI-informed, not MBTI-labeled)
- **Influence** — How much their opinion shapes other players (0.1 lurker → 1.0 KOL)

## Persona 1: Casual Newcomer (FTUE Focus)

**Profile:** First mobile game session on commute. Found the game via ad or friend's recommendation. 3 minutes of attention before deciding if it's worth keeping. Age 18-35, plays 2-3 casual games regularly, deletes apps that confuse them in the first minute.

**Stance:** Neutral-to-positive (0.0 to +0.3). Willing to try but quick to abandon. Not emotionally invested yet. Will not give a second chance if the first impression is bad.

**Behavior type:** First-impression driven. Taps the biggest, brightest button. Skips text longer than 2 lines. Ignores tutorials if a "skip" button exists. Follows the most obvious path only. Judges entirely by visuals and feel in the first 30 seconds. Shares screenshots to friends if impressed, silently deletes if not.

**Influence:** 0.2-0.3 (individual sharing). Their opinion reaches 5-10 people via word of mouth but they don't post publicly.

**Spending:** Will not pay in the first session. Might consider a purchase after Day 3 if emotionally invested. Price sensitive above $5.

**Key question at every moment:** "Do I get it? Is this worth my time?"

**Simulates:** D0 first 3-5 minutes. FTUE quality. First impression churn risk.

## Persona 2: Interested Returner (Day 2-3)

**Profile:** Liked the game yesterday. Opening it for the second time with 10-15 minutes available. Remembers the core mechanic but not the menus. Has told one friend about the game but hasn't recommended it yet.

**Stance:** Supportive (+0.3 to +0.5). Gave the game a second chance, which means session 1 worked. Now looking for confirmation that their time investment will pay off.

**Behavior type:** Verification-seeking. Checks "what's new" before replaying. Notices missing quality-of-life features (no save indicator, confusing navigation). Sensitive to repetition — "wait, this is the same thing as yesterday?" Reads short notifications but skips long text. Starts forming opinions about the game's long-term value.

**Influence:** 0.3-0.4 (active recommender). If convinced, they tell 3-5 friends with specific reasons. If disappointed, they say "I tried it, it's okay" and the conversation ends.

**Spending:** Notices IAP prompts but will not buy unless there's a clear "I want MORE of this" feeling. Evaluates value: "Is $5 worth it for what I'm getting?"

**Key question at every moment:** "Is there more here, or was yesterday the whole game?"

**Simulates:** D2-D3 retention. Content depth. Second session hook.

## Persona 3: Dedicated Player (Day 7)

**Profile:** Daily player, 20-30 minute sessions. Has invested time and possibly money. Knows the systems. Part of the game's subreddit or Discord but mostly reads, rarely posts. Has a preferred playstyle and is starting to optimize.

**Stance:** Supportive but critical (+0.2 to +0.4). Invested enough to care about problems rather than just leaving. Will file bug reports. Gets frustrated by issues that feel disrespectful of their time.

**Behavior type:** Systematic evaluator. Explores all menus eventually. Notices economy imbalances (earning too slow/fast). Spots progression walls that feel artificial. Compares to other games in the genre methodically: "Game X does this better." Writes detailed feedback if asked.

**Influence:** 0.4-0.5 (community participant). Posts in Discord, contributes to wiki, answers questions. Their detailed criticism carries weight with other dedicated players.

**Spending:** Has evaluated the IAP. Will buy if value is clear. Sensitive to pay-to-win signals. Calculates cost-per-hour of entertainment.

**Key question at every moment:** "Am I still growing, or am I on a treadmill?"

**Simulates:** D7 retention. Mid-game economy. Progression pacing. Social feature need.

## Persona 4: Hardcore Optimizer (Day 30+)

**Profile:** Min-maxing everything. Has tested multiple strategies. Reads wikis, watches guides, knows the meta. Plays 1-2 hours per session. Has strong opinions about what the game should be.

**Stance:** Skeptical-to-critical (-0.2 to +0.1). Loves the game's potential but frustrated by balance issues, lack of endgame, or "casual-pandering" changes. Will argue passionately about design decisions.

**Behavior type:** Analytical breaker. Tries to break systems. Finds exploits (infinite resource loops, stat overflow). Tests edge cases ("what if I sell everything?"). Pushes difficulty limits. Posts detailed analysis: "If you do X, Y happens. Here's the math." Writes build guides and tier lists. Nitpicks balance at decimal precision.

**Influence:** 0.6-0.7 (opinion leader). Their build guides and analysis posts shape the meta. Other players copy their strategies. When they say "this is broken," the community listens.

**Spending:** Either whale (>$100) or principled F2P. Judges IAP by competitive advantage, not cosmetics. Will leave 1-star review if pay-to-win is detected.

**Key question at every moment:** "Can I master this, or is it just grind?"

**Simulates:** D30+ retention. Endgame health. Balance exploits. Meta-game depth.

## Persona 5: Content Creator / Streamer

**Profile:** Evaluating the game as content. 5K-50K subscriber range (mid-tier = highest per-viewer influence because they feel peer-like). Plays with an audience in mind. Looking for "clip moments" — 15-30 second highlights that work on social media.

**Stance:** Enthusiastic but performative (+0.4 to +0.6). Wants the game to be good because good games make good content. Will amplify positives dramatically but will also publicly roast problems for entertainment value.

**Behavior type:** Entertainment-first evaluator. Asks "would I clip this?" every 30 seconds. Rates: dramatic wins, funny failures, impressive skill expression, visual spectacle. Penalizes: boring stretches that lose viewers, confusing moments requiring too much context to explain, long loading/menus. May exaggerate reactions for the camera. Their coverage in hours 4-8 after launch shapes community perception more than journalist reviews.

**Influence:** 0.7-0.9 (amplifier). Their stream creates the first viral moments. A positive first-play stream can drive 1000+ wishlist additions. A negative "this is boring" stream can kill launch momentum.

**Spending:** Irrelevant — they often get review keys. But their audience watches spending decisions closely.

**Key question at every moment:** "Would I clip this? Would my audience enjoy watching this?"

**Simulates:** Virality potential. Spectator experience. Streamability. Launch window amplification.

## Persona 6: Returning Player (Day 90, Lapsed)

**Profile:** Played actively for 2 weeks, then life happened. Haven't opened the game in months. Checking back because of an update notification, a friend mentioned it, or boredom.

**Stance:** Neutral-to-negative (-0.1 to +0.1). Left once, which means something wasn't strong enough to keep them. Re-entry friction will confirm their decision to leave. A smooth "welcome back" might rekindle engagement.

**Behavior type:** Impatient scanner. Looks for "what's new" immediately. Judges by first 60 seconds whether coming back was worth it. Will not re-learn complex systems. Overwhelmed by: too many new features, missed events, expired rewards, "catch-up" mechanics that feel like homework. One bad experience = permanent uninstall.

**Influence:** 0.1-0.2 (silent majority). Represents the largest segment of any game's player base (lapsed players always outnumber active players). Their re-engagement success/failure determines long-term revenue but they almost never post publicly about it.

**Spending:** Very price-sensitive on return. Needs a "welcome back" deal or clear value demonstration before opening wallet again.

**Key question at every moment:** "Is it worth coming back, or have I fallen too far behind?"

**Simulates:** Reactivation UX. Catch-up mechanics. Lapsed player churn. Long-term retention recovery.

## Persona 7: Genre Critic (Expert Evaluator)

**Profile:** Has played 200+ games in this genre. Writes reviews or participates in genre-specific communities. Evaluates this game against the full history of the genre. Uses domain vocabulary precisely (e.g., for Roguelikes: "build diversity," "permadeath," "procedural generation," "run variance").

**Stance:** Skeptical (-0.3 to 0.0). "Prove this deserves to exist in a genre with [established competitor]." Will compare every system to the genre's best examples. Praise is earned, not given.

**Behavior type:** Precedent-driven judge. Immediately compares to genre peers: "Hades did this better," "This is basically [game] but worse." Tests whether the game earns its genre label. Checks system depth at expert level. Will find and publicize any claims that don't hold up ("they said 80+ builds but only 3 are viable"). Posts long-form analysis.

**Influence:** 0.5-0.7 (genre authority). Their endorsement grants genre legitimacy. Their criticism defines the narrative for the game's first week. Other reviewers reference their analysis.

**Spending:** $15-30 range is acceptable for genre quality. Will refund if the game doesn't deliver on genre promises within 2 hours.

**Key question at every moment:** "What does this game add to the genre that [best competitor] doesn't already do?"

**Simulates:** Genre differentiation. Expert-level system depth. Competitive positioning. Review narrative.

## Custom Persona

If the user describes a specific player type not covered above, adopt their description. Ask for:
- **Profile:** Context — when/where/why are they playing? Age range, gaming history?
- **Stance:** Where on the support-to-skepticism spectrum? (-1.0 to +1.0)
- **Behavior type:** How do they process games? (analytical, emotional, social, visual-first, speedrunner, completionist)
- **Influence:** How many people does their opinion reach? (0.1-1.0)
- **Key question** they ask themselves every 30 seconds

## Stance Distribution Guide

When the user wants to test community reception (not just individual experience), use these realistic distributions:

| Scenario | Strong Support | Support | Neutral | Opposing | Strong Opposing |
|----------|---------------|---------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| New indie (no track record) | 10% | 20% | 40% | 20% | 10% |
| Established franchise DLC | 5% | 20% | 30% | 35% | 10% |
| F2P with gacha announced | 5% | 15% | 25% | 35% | 20% |
| Beloved IP adaptation | 35% | 25% | 15% | 15% | 10% |
| Early Access launch | 15% | 25% | 35% | 15% | 10% |

These distributions help calibrate severity: if 40% of the audience is neutral, a confusing FTUE loses the entire middle.
